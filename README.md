# What Are the Challenges of Coaching Youth Basketball?

**Author:** Isaac Covarrubias
**Course:** Sociology 102: Introduction to Research Methods
**Institution:** College of the Canyons
**Term:** Spring 2019

---

## Overview

This repository contains the research project I completed for my Introduction to Research Methods course at College of the Canyons. The study investigates the challenges faced by volunteer youth basketball coaches in the City of Santa Clarita's recreational league, using a mixed-methods approach combining survey data and in-depth interviews.

The research was motivated by my professional role recruiting and retaining volunteer coaches for the City of Santa Clarita, and my personal experience as a volunteer youth basketball coach for a non-profit travel basketball organization.

**Research Question:** *What are the challenges in coaching youth basketball?*

---

## Key Findings

- The most prominent challenge identified by coaches was **time commitment**, followed closely by **job/life balance** and **managing parents**
- Contrary to the initial assumption that parents would be the single biggest challenge, time commitment ranked first among survey respondents
- Three hypotheses testing correlations between age, challenge scores, and outside influence susceptibility were all rejected — suggesting these factors are more complex and warrant further research
- Qualitative interviews revealed four major themes: **personal experiences**, **adapting to new players**, **parents**, and **the issue of travel ball**

---

## Study Design

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sample size | N = 50 survey respondents; 5 interview participants |
| Population | Head and assistant coaches in the City of Santa Clarita 2019 winter youth basketball season |
| Data collection | Mailed/emailed surveys + in-person interviews (~1 hour each) |
| Analysis | Univariate statistics, bivariate crosstabulations, Pearson correlations, thematic qualitative analysis |
| Scales used | Outside Influence Susceptibility Scale (α = .767); Challenges Scale (α = .846) |

## Context & Limitations

This study was conducted as a first-semester research methods course project and should be treated as a **pilot study**. Key limitations include:

- The sample was limited to one recreational league in Santa Clarita, CA
- Travel ball coaches were not surveyed, representing a gap for future research
- Survey instrument was designed early in the learning process and could be refined
- All three inferential hypotheses were rejected, indicating the need for broader follow-up research
